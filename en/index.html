The album "dot" - a beginning not an end

Call it chance, but we prefer to think of it as fate - some things were meant to be. Chance's album "dot" wasn't planned by any means. "dot" was set in motion from as an impromptu meeting with old friends. Reconnecting, we reawakened the meaning and value of our friendship and music, and our powerful connection to God.

As is often the case with friendship, life's complicated path can lead us to different destinations. This was especially true for the band, Chance. As our lives exponentially expanded our music was regrettably relegated to second place. They say the measure of a true friendship is one that can survive the test of time and distance. In 2015, meeting for an impromptu coffee, at Tower Cafe in Sacramento, was like coming home. We reminisced over the happy times we shared, both as friends and as musicians - how our music brought joy to our lives, strengthened our friendship and connection to God. This joy is so immense that we want to share it. With the love and support of our families we put a "dot" at the end of the chapter of our separate pathways.

Taking the Chance - Since 1996

Chance came together as a band in 1996. We pooled our individual musical talents, and combined faith and love of Christ, to create music that would inspire and bring joy to those who listen. Our music and love of the Lord has made us brothers, enriched our lives and faith, and has led those we touched to Christ. We hope that you will find the same joy. Take the Chance and listen.

Serg Shemyakin

I was born in Riga, Latvia. At age six my family moved to Odessa, Ukraine and from there we immigrated to the USA. I first picked up a guitar at age fourteen and at once was passionate about playing. I knew that music was my destiny. I composed my first song "Years Like the Clouds" a year later, and the rest is history! My passion for music grew stronger as it combined with my faith in Christ. As He led me through my darkest times, I used music to express my devotion and feelings. For me a song is like a cry from the heart; the thoughts in my mind come to life through my composition and music.

I'm originally from Odessa, Ukraine and later moved to the USA. I was fortunate that in my youth Jesus Christ found me. My life has been infinitely richer through my unfaltering faith. Through Him I found my love of music and my closest friends. My path to music came from following in the footsteps of my brothers who played guitars. Soon after we started our band. Our hope and goal is that through our music we praise His name and touch souls with our musical expression of His greatness.

Vadim Kapakly

I was born in Odessa, Ukraine. Surprisingly, at first, music was not my passion by any means. During my first five years of piano lessons I hated it! As time passed, and my connection to God strengthened, my love of music grew. I found a way to express my faith through making music. I am grateful to God to leading me down His path and the connection to my brothers in CHANCE. He has led me away from sin and to a life of servitude of the Lord.

Viktor Okunev

I was born in Russia. I started playing the saxophone in a jazz band at the beginning of seventh grade. Growing up surrounded by church friends I had plenty of time to practice my music; youth groups gave me the chance to sing in worship groups. The "Three Musketeers from Odessa" - also known as Serg, Vadim and Aleksey - started a band in 1996. I was thrilled to be asked to join their band in 1999 when they came to USA. Almost twenty years later, we are still together with the same united goal - to bring inspiration, faith and encouragement to the world through our musical expression of His love.
